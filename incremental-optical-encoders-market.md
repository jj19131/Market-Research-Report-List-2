<p><p>Incremental optical encoders can be categorized into different types based on their internal design and output signals. Type A encoders produce a single square wave output signal that increases in frequency with rotation, providing high resolution but limited functionality. Type B encoders feature two square wave output signals that are 90 degrees out of phase with each other, allowing for improved accuracy and direction detection. Type Z encoders include an additional index channel that provides a reference point for absolute position determination, enhancing precision and reliability.</p></p>
