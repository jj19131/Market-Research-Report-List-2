<p><p>Thermophotovoltaic cells are categorized into Epitaxial Method Cells and Non-Epitaxial Method Cells. Epitaxial method cells are manufactured using a high-temperature deposition process, resulting in higher efficiency but also higher production costs. Non-epitaxial method cells, on the other hand, are produced using a simpler and more cost-effective method, although they may have lower efficiency. Both types of cells aim to convert heat into electricity using a semiconductor material in order to generate renewable energy.</p></p>
