<p><ul><li>Liquid Membrane</li><li>PVC Membrane</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Sodium ion selective electrodes are used for measuring the concentration of sodium ions in solutions. There are two main types of such electrodes: liquid membrane and PVC membrane. Liquid membrane electrodes use a solvent containing a selective ionophore, while PVC membrane electrodes have a polyvinyl chloride membrane with selective ionophores embedded in it. Both types of electrodes operate by selectively binding sodium ions and producing a potential difference, which can be measured to determine the concentration of sodium in the solution.</p></p>
