<p><p>Whole wheat flour is available in various types based on its intended use in baking. Bread flour has a high protein content, making it ideal for making yeast breads that require strong gluten development. Pastry flour has a lower protein content, resulting in tender and flaky pastries. Cake flour has the lowest protein content, producing delicate and soft cakes. Other types of whole wheat flour cater to specific baking needs, such as self-rising flour for leavening or whole wheat pastry flour for light, airy pastries.</p></p>
