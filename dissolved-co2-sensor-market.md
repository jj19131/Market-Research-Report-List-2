<p><p>Dissolved CO2 sensors are devices used to measure the concentration of carbon dioxide in a liquid. There are three main types of dissolved CO2 sensors in the market. Potentiometric sensors measure CO2 concentration based on changes in electrical potential. Thermal conductivity sensors detect CO2 by measuring changes in heat conduction. Infrared optics sensors use infrared light to analyze CO2 levels. Each type of sensor has its own advantages and is suitable for different applications based on factors like accuracy, sensitivity, and cost efficiency.</p></p>
