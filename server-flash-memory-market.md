<p><p>Server Flash Memory-NAND Flash Market consists of two types of NAND Flash memory: SLC (Single-Level Cell) and MLC (Multi-Level Cell). SLC offers faster read/write speeds and greater endurance, making it ideal for high-performance applications. MLC, on the other hand, provides higher storage capacities at a lower cost but with slightly slower performance and reduced lifespan. Both types are widely used in server applications, with SLC typically preferred for critical, high-speed tasks, while MLC is suitable for more cost-sensitive storage needs.</p></p>
