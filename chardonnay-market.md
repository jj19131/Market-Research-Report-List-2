<p><p>Chardonnay wine is typically categorized into two main market types: Oaked and Unoaked. Oaked Chardonnay is aged in oak barrels, giving it a rich and creamy texture with flavors of vanilla, spice, and butterscotch. On the other hand, Unoaked Chardonnay is fermented in stainless steel tanks, resulting in a crisp and fresh wine with vibrant fruit flavors and a lighter body. Both types appeal to different preferences, with Oaked Chardonnay being favored by those who enjoy a more complex and decadent taste, while Unoaked Chardonnay is preferred by those who prefer a lighter and fruit-forward wine.</p></p>
